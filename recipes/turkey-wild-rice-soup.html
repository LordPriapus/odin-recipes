Turkey Wild Rice Soup Recipe

Description

For those cold winter nights what better dinner to have than a hot turkey soup?
This soup will warm your home and your heart. Save Thanksgiving and Christmas turkey
leftovers just so that you can make this yummy soup.

Ingredients

  • ⅔ cup uncooked wild rice
  • 2 cups water
  • 6 tablespoons butter
  • ¼ cup finely chopped celery
  • ¼ cup finely chopped onion
  • ⅓ cup all-purpose flour
  • 4 cups turkey broth
  • ⅓ cup shredded carrot
  • 2 cups chopped cooked turkey
  • ½ teaspoon kosher salt, or to taste
  • ½ teaspoon ground black pepper, or to taste
  • ¼ cup chopped slivered almonds
  • ½ teaspoon lemon juice
  • ¾ cup half-and-half cream

Steps

  1. Bring the wild rice and water to a boil in a saucepan. Reduce heat to medium-low,cover,
    and simmer until the rice is tender but not mushy, 40 to 45 minutes.
  2. Drain off any excess liquid, fluff the rice with a fork, and cook uncovered 5 minutes more. Set the cooked rice aside.
  3. Melt the butter in a soup pot over medium heat. Cook and stir the onion and celery until the onion is translucent, about 5 minutes.
    Stir in the flour, and cook until it turns a yellowish-brown color
  4. Gradually whisk in the turkey stock until no lumps of flour remain. Stir in the carrot. Bring the mixture to a simmer, and cook, whisking
    constantly, unti the stock is thick and smooth and the carrot is tender, about 2 more minutes.
  5. Stir in the wild rice, turkey, salt, pepper, and almonds. Return to a simmer, and cook 2 more minutes to heat the ingredients.
  6. Stir in the lemon juice and half-and-half; bring the soup almost to a boil, and serve hot.
